What is ISO 50001?

ISO 50001:2018 is an international standard that specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and improving an energy management system (EnMS). The purpose is to enable organizations to follow a systematic approach in achieving continual improvement of energy performance, including energy efficiency, energy use, and energy consumption.

This ENMS Demo Platform is designed to support organizations in meeting ISO 50001 requirements by providing comprehensive tools for energy monitoring, analysis, and reporting.

Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) Cycle

ISO 50001 is based on the continuous improvement framework known as the PDCA cycle. Our platform supports each phase:

Plan

Define energy baseline, objectives, and action plans using historical analysis and performance metrics.

Do

Implement energy management actions with real-time monitoring and automated data collection.

Check

Analyze results through interactive dashboards, EnPI tracking, and performance comparisons.

Act

Take corrective actions based on insights and continue the improvement cycle with ML predictions.

Energy Performance Indicators (EnPIs)

The platform automatically calculates and tracks multiple EnPIs required by ISO 50001:

Supported EnPIs

  • Total Energy Consumption: Absolute energy use over time (kWh)
  • Specific Energy Consumption (SEC): Energy per unit of production (kWh/unit)
  • Energy Intensity: Energy use normalized by production volume or hours
  • Peak Demand: Maximum power draw during operations (kW)
  • Load Factor: Ratio of average to peak energy demand
  • Energy Cost per Unit: Financial energy performance indicator ($/unit)
  • Carbon Intensity: CO₂ emissions per unit of production (kgCO₂e/unit)
